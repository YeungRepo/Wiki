Brief Bio

I received my B.S. in Mathematics, magna cum laude and with University Honors, in 2010 from Brigham Young University. While at BYU, I was fortunate to receive a College of Physical and Mathematical Sciences Choose to Give Scholarship, IDeA Labs Research Fellowship and an Office of Research and Creative Arts (ORCA) Grant to study network modeling, reduction, and representation problems with Dr. Sean Warnick. I also have had the privilege of working with Dr. Sandip Roy, director of the Dynamical Networks Group at Washington State University, Dr. Jorge Goncalves in the Control Group at Cambridge University, and Dr. Henrik Sandberg at the Royal Institute of Technology.

I am currently at the California Institute of Technology, pursuing a Ph.D in Control and Dynamical Systems on a Charles Lee Powell Foundation Fellowship. I am working with professor (http://www.cds.caltech.edu/~murray Richard Murray). Throughout my PhD, I have been supported generously by a National Defense and Science Engineering Graduate (NDSEG) Fellowship, a Charles Lee Powell Foundation Fellowship, and a Kanel Foundation Fellowship.
